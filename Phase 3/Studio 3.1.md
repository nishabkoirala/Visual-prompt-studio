Prompt 1 

Historical Theme: Art as a divine power

Historical period: Ancient Egypt(c. 2500 BCE)

Artwork Inspiration: Khafre with the Falcon God Horus

Medium: Stone Sculpture

Visual Prompt

Create a seated statue of an Egyptian pharaoh from dark stone. The figure is shown facing forward in a stiff, upright position with a straight back and both hands resting on the knees. The body should look strong but calm, with no movement. The face is idealized, with smooth features and a serious expression.
Behind the head, include a falcon with its wings wrapped around the ruler, showing protection. The throne is decorated with carved symbols and straight lines that create balanced and order. The surface of the sculpture is smooth and polished. The figure is slightly larger than life-size to show importance.
The overall design should feel stable, balanced, and timeless, with strong symmetry and very little emotion or movement.

Historical Purpose

This sculpture was made to display that the pharaoh was not only a political leader, but god as well. Egyptians thought that the pharaoh was close to gods and helped maintain order. The rigid pose, symmetry and solid shape represent stability and control, which were political themes. the falcon symbolises a god watching over the king, showing religious ideas. The sculpture was also created for a tomb or a temple so that the king could have the eternal life. It demonstrates the relationship between religion, politics, and culture, and the resources used to create monuments to display power and belief
